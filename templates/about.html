The formation of the group grew out of a meeting Gandalf had with Thorin in Bree which kindled Thorin's interest in recapturing his long lost family inheritance. (Thorin's grandfather had been the King under the Mountain when Smaug came and took it.) Remembering that he had once known an adventurous Hobbit on his travels in the Shire, Gandalf decided to add Bilbo to their company because he knew that stealth and cunning were preferable to force. Gandalf also believed that someone like Bilbo could keep the sometimes prideful and stubborn Dwarves from rash action. The superstitious Dwarves also considered thirteen to be an unlucky number, and as Gandalf had planned to leave on other business, welcomed a fourteenth to fill into their party.

Thorin Oakenshield

Thorin, born in TA 2746, was the heir to the throne of Erebor. After Smaug drove his people into exile in TA 2770, Thorin became a fierce warrior, earning his epithet "Oakenshield" in the Battle of Azanulbizar (TA 2799). He led Thorin's Company to reclaim the Lonely Mountain and its treasure. Thorin's resolve was tested by the Arkenstone, and he ultimately perished in the Battle of Five Armies defending Erebor with honor.

Fili and Kili

Fíli and Kíli, Thorin's nephews, joined him in reclaiming Erebor. Known for their sharp eyes, they found the Front Porch and scouted Ravenhill. They defended Thorin with their lives in the Battle of Five Armies, where both fell with honor. Their loyalty and courage made them memorable among Thorin's Company.

Óin

Óin, skilled with fire-making, fought at Azanulbizar before joining Thorin's quest. Wearing a brown hood, he played a vital role in the journey, preparing campfires and enduring many trials. He survived the Battle of Five Armies and later settled in Erebor under King Dáin II.

Glóin

Glóin, born in TA 2783, fought in Azanulbizar and later joined Thorin's quest with his brother Óin. Known for his fire-starting skills, Glóin survived the Battle of Five Armies. He settled in Erebor, where his son Gimli was born and later became a key figure in the War of the Ring.

Balin

Balin, a respected elder, fought at Azanulbizar and accompanied Thráin on a failed attempt to reclaim Erebor. In TA 2941, he joined Thorin's Company and played music at Bag End. Balin survived the Battle of Five Armies and lived under King Dáin II at Erebor.

Dwalin

Dwalin, a loyal warrior, fought at Azanulbizar and joined Thráin’s ill-fated expedition. In TA 2941, he became the first dwarf to arrive at Bag End, wearing a green hood. Dwalin survived the Battle of Five Armies and settled in Erebor, sharing in its riches.

Ori

Ori, a scribe and warrior, joined Thorin's Company in TA 2941. He survived trials with Goblins, Trolls, and Smaug before fighting in the Battle of Five Armies. Ori later made his home in Erebor under King Dáin II Ironfoot, living in prosperity.

Dori

Dori, protective and strong, was known for carrying Bilbo in the Misty Mountains. A skilled flutist, he joined Thorin's quest in TA 2941. Dori survived the Battle of Five Armies and later lived in Erebor under Dáin II, enjoying its wealth.

Nori

Nori, an adventurous dwarf, joined Thorin's Company in TA 2941 alongside his brothers Dori and Ori. Known for his flute-playing, he faced Goblins, Trolls, and Smaug. Nori fought at the Battle of Five Armies and settled in Erebor under King Dáin II.

Bifur

Bifur, descended from Khazad-dûm dwarves, was a clarinet-playing warrior in Thorin's Company. Despite his injuries, he endured the quest's dangers and fought valiantly at the Battle of Five Armies. He later retired to Erebor under Dáin II Ironfoot.

Bofur

Bofur, Bifur’s cousin, joined Thorin’s quest to reclaim Erebor. Known for his jovial spirit, he endured many trials on the journey. Bofur fought in the Battle of Five Armies and lived a prosperous life in Erebor under King Dáin II.

Bombur

Bombur, brother of Bofur, was known for his size and appetite. He joined Thorin’s quest and survived Goblins, Trolls, and Smaug. Bombur fought in the Battle of Five Armies and lived in Erebor, sharing in its treasure and peace.

Gandalf

Gandalf, the Grey Wizard, initiated Thorin’s quest, selecting Bilbo as the Company’s burglar. He saved the group from Trolls and Goblins, wielding Glamdring. Gandalf’s wisdom guided Thorin’s Company through many perils to Erebor.

Bilbo Baggins

Bilbo, a Hobbit of Bag End, was reluctantly recruited as the burglar for Thorin's quest. Using his wit, he outsmarted Trolls, escaped Goblins, and retrieved the Arkenstone. Bilbo’s courage was crucial in reclaiming Erebor, though he ultimately returned to the Shire.
